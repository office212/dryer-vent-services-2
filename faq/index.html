Frequently Asked Questions

Answers about cleaning schedules, pricing, safety and what to expect on the day of service.

How often should I clean my dryer vent?

Most homes need a full dryer vent cleaning once a year. If you have a large family or run many loads each week, every 6-9 months is recommended.

How long does a standard cleaning take?

A typical dryer vent cleaning takes about 30-45 minutes. Longer runs, roof exits or damaged vents can take more time, but most visits are under an hour.

Will my home get dirty during the cleaning?

No. We use a sealed vacuum system and professional brushing tools that keep lint and dust contained. The work area is left neat and clean when we finish.

What are the signs that I need a cleaning?

You may need a cleaning if you notice:

  • Clothes taking longer than one cycle to dry
  • The laundry room feels hotter than usual
  • A burning or hot smell when the dryer runs
  • The dryer shuts off mid-cycle
  • It has been more than a year since your last cleaning

Do you clean the dryer itself or only the vent?

We clean the entire vent route from the back of the dryer to the outside termination, including the exhaust hood. We do not repair dryers themselves - we focus on ducts, vents and airflow.

What do you charge for dryer vent cleaning?

We use honest, flat pricing with no surprises at the door. Pricing depends mainly on the floor and where the vent exits:

  • Basement / 1st Floor: $150
  • 2nd Floor: $200
  • 3rd Floor or Roof Exit: $250

This covers a full cleaning of the vent. If we discover broken parts or unsafe venting, we will explain the options before doing any repair work.

Do you charge extra for bird nests or heavy lint?

No. If the vent is just very clogged with lint or even has a bird nest blocking the hood, the price stays the same as the flat cleaning rate for that floor.

How are repairs and new vent installations priced?

Repairs and new vent installations are priced by estimate, based on what we find on site. Things like replacing crushed ducts, re-routing vents or installing a brand new outside hood are quoted clearly before we start.

Do I need to be home during the service?

Yes. Someone needs to be home to give us access to the dryer, confirm the location of the vent exit and test the airflow together when we are done.

Can a clogged dryer vent really cause a fire?

Yes. Lint is extremely flammable. A clogged vent traps heat and prevents the dryer from exhausting properly, which can overheat the system and create a fire risk. Regular cleaning greatly reduces that risk.

Will cleaning the vent help my dryer work better?

In most homes, yes. A clean vent improves airflow so the dryer does not need to run as long or work as hard. This usually means faster dry times, less wear on the machine and lower energy use.
