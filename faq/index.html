General cleaning questions

How often should I clean my dryer vent?

Most homes need a full dryer vent cleaning once a year. If you do a lot of laundry (large family, many loads each week), every 6–9 months is recommended.

How long does a standard cleaning take?

A normal dryer vent cleaning takes about 30–45 minutes. If the vent is very long, partially blocked, or exits from a higher floor, it can take longer – we stay until airflow is truly improved.

Will my house get dirty during the cleaning?

No. We use a sealed vacuum system and professional brushes designed for dryer vents. Lint and dust are collected directly into our equipment, not into your laundry room.

What are the signs that I need a cleaning?
  • Clothes take more than one cycle to dry
  • The laundry room or dryer feels unusually hot
  • You notice a burning or hot smell while the dryer runs
  • The exterior vent flap barely opens or has lint around it
  • It has been more than a year since your last cleaning
Do you clean the dryer itself or only the vent?

We focus on the vent system – from the back of the dryer to the exterior hood. We do not repair or open the dryer appliance itself. If we see an issue with the dryer, we will let you know.

Do I need to be home during the service?

Yes. Someone needs to be home to give us access to the dryer and laundry area, and to confirm that the dryer is venting properly after the cleaning or repair.

Safety & performance

Can a clogged dryer vent really cause a fire?

Yes. Lint is extremely flammable. A clogged vent traps heat, which can overheat the dryer and increase the risk of a fire. Regular cleaning greatly reduces that risk and keeps the system running safely.

Will cleaning the vent help my dryer work better?

In most cases, yes. When the vent is blocked, hot air cannot escape, so clothes stay wet, cycles run longer, and the dryer works harder. After cleaning, many customers see shorter drying times and better performance.
